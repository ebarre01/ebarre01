Displaced: Exploring the racialized impacts of gentrification

While the disparate impacts of gentrification are widely documented, understanding the extent to which communities of color are being displaced remains relatively unclear. This interactive map explores demographic trends in Lexington, KY between 1990-2018.

Dimensions of neighborhood transition and affordable housing, Lexington, KY

Securing safe and affordable housing is one of the most pressing issues currently faced by cities across the world. This interactive CARTO map layers several indicators of affordable housing, gentrification and urban change.

Code Enforcement: A weapon of gentrification?

While code enforcement violations ideally ensure safe housing conditions, they are increasing scrutnized as an adminstrative tool that spurs gentrification and unfairly benefits property developers. This interactive web map explores patterns of code enforcement violations in Lexington, KY.

Housing Affordability

Rental housing within the United States is increasingly unaffordable. In fact, a 2016 report by the National Low Income Housing Coalition (NLIHC) found that in no U.S. state, metropolitan area, or county could a full-time worker earning the prevailing minimum wage afford a modest two-bedroom apartment. This interactive web map explores the median rent in counties across the nation.

New Zealnd Tourism

With active volcanoes, snowcapped peaks, glacier lakes, and sandy beaches, New Zealand offers visitors a never ending list of new adventures waiting to be explored. Inspired by the New Zealand tourist board, this web map shows a perfect seven day road trip featuring some of the North Island's most famous attractions.

Syracuse Parks & Amenities, 2019

The city of Syracuse offers its residents an abundance of green space. But, are these parks accessible and equally connected to other city amenities? This static map explores this relationship, with the intent of finding trails and amenities that are within walking distance of public parks.

About me

Hi there, I'm Emily Barrett. I'm a geographer specializing in affordable housing, community-engaged mapping and urban planning. My work broadly explores the opportunities and challenges of using maps and their associated data-driven narratives within politically contentious discussions of gentrification and urban development. I am currently a Ph.D. student at Vanderbilt University.
